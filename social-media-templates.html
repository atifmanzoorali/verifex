E — LinkedIn Caption Rules What to write in the post body when sharing these templates
Length & Format
  • Optimal length: 900–1,300 characters. Long enough to tell the story, short enough to hold attention.
  • First line is the hook. It shows before "see more". Make it a question or a sharp statement.
  • Line breaks matter. One idea per line. Whitespace increases readability by 40%.
  • No wall of text. Max 2 sentences per paragraph.
Voice & Tone
  • Contractions allowed: "you don't" not "you do not". Write how you talk.
  • No hashtags. Verifex rule. They reduce reach on LinkedIn in 2025–2026.
  • No emojis. The design carries the visual weight. The caption stays clean.
  • End with a question when the goal is engagement. End with a link when the goal is traffic.
CTA Patterns
  • Docs: "Full API reference at verifex.dev/docs"
  • GitHub: "Star the repo — link in comments."
  • Engagement: "What does your validation flow look like?"
  • Announcement: "Shipped today. What do you want to see next?"
  • Never: "Like and share if you found this useful."

D — X Caption Rules X moves faster than LinkedIn — different rules apply
Length & Format
  • 280 character limit on standard posts. Do not exceed it — cropping kills impact.
  • Hook in the first line. Most people won't expand. The first sentence must carry the whole idea.
  • One idea per post. Save nuance for the thread. The standalone post must land on its own.
  • Short sentences win. Under 12 words per sentence where possible.
Voice & Tone
  • Sharper than LinkedIn. Cut every word that isn't earning its place.
  • No hashtags. Verifex rule. They signal low quality on X in 2025–2026.
  • No emojis. The image carries the visual. The text stays clean.
  • Contractions always. "You don't" not "you do not". Reads faster.
  • Second person only. "You", "your", never "we found that users…"
Thread Rules
  • Thread cover image = tweet 1. It must be a complete hook — not a teaser.
  • Number your tweets inside the thread: "1/ The problem. 2/ Why it matters…"
  • Last tweet = CTA. Always. Either a link, a follow ask, or a question to reply.
  • Max 10 tweets per thread. Past that, publish a blog post instead.
  • Reply to your own thread 2 hours after posting to boost reach.

F — Instagram Caption Rules Instagram captions read differently — shorter visible text, line breaks matter more
Length & Visibility
  • First 125 characters show before "more". The hook must live there.
  • Optimal length: 150–300 characters for feed posts. Long captions on Instagram are ignored.
  • Stories have no captions. All communication is in the image — keep text on the template itself brief and readable at a glance.
  • Line breaks as punctuation. One line. Gap. Next line. It reads vertically on mobile.
Voice & Format
  • No hashtags. Verifex rule. They reduce organic reach on Instagram in 2025–2026.
  • No emojis. The template carries the visual weight.
  • Contractions always. "You don't" not "you do not".
  • End with a CTA on every post — "Link in bio" is the standard call to action on Instagram.
  • Never say "double tap" or "smash the like button". Direct and clean.
Stories & Highlights
  • Stories live 24 hours. Use for time-sensitive content — announcements, events, behind-the-scenes.
  • Save every announcement story to the matching Highlight (Updates, API, FAQ, etc.).
  • Link-in-bio story should be posted every time you update the link. Do not assume followers saw it.
  • Carousels get 3× more reach than single posts on Instagram. Use them for educational content.

D — How to Use This Kit Taking these templates into Canva or Figma
01
Set up your token library
Create a colour library in Canva or Figma using the 4 background variants and the semantic tokens from DESIGN.md. Name them exactly as defined — Dark, Teal, Cream, White. Never use a colour that is not in the system.
02
Build one master template per format
Create one frame per format at the correct pixel size — 1200×627 for LinkedIn landscape, 1080×1080 for square, 1080×1920 for stories. Then duplicate it 4 times and apply each colour variant. This becomes your source-of-truth template set.
03
Swap content, keep structure
For each post, choose the content type (Quote, Stat, Announcement, etc.), choose the variant from the usage guide in Phase 1, then replace only the text. Never move the logo, never change the font weight, never add elements that are not in the template. The structure is the brand.
